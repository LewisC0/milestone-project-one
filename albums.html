Album Reviews

Joy Division - Unknown Pleasures

Joy Division's seminal debut album. Unknown Pleasures by Joy Division is a front to back masterpiece of Post Punk and Proto Goth Rock. The band hit out of the park with theeir first release, a dark and atmospheric record which inspired multiple new genres of music. Mixing sounds of the booming punk scene at the time in the UK, with Ian Curtis' own influences in David Bowie and The Velvet Underground, the album has such a killer sound which still sounds incredibly unique to this day... read more >

Opeth - Blackwater Park

Opeth's haunting and beautiful progressive death metal record which rocked the Swedish and International metal scene in 2001, Blackwater Park is a masterpiece of an album which bounces from incredibly aggressive highs to soft yet haunting lows in such a seamless way. Mikael Åkerfeldt masterful juxtaposing vocal styles, and unique composition style, make for one of the best metal album of the 2000s... read more >

Black Sabbath - Paranoid

Black Sabbath's sophmore release which changed heavy music forever. On the 18th of September 1970, the Metal Genre was born with the release of this album in the UK and later on January 7th 1971 in the States. Tony Iommi's heavy multi-tracked guitar work, Geezer Butler's wild bass lines, Bill Wards dominating drum fills and Ozzy Osbourne's untrained yet powerful vocals, were unlike anything heard at the time... read more >

Metallica - Master Of Puppets

Metallica's incredibly influencial third album, often considered the peak of Metallica's career in the 1980s, as well as one of the best metal records of all time. A monumnetal achievement in the genre of Thrash Metal, which manages to combine the brutality of the 80s Thrash Metal scene with some softer Prog influences throughout. Every member of the band was working at the height of their game during this album's production, providing a 54 minute long masterclass of guitar's, bass, drum's and vocal's... read more >

Tool - Ænima

The second full length studio album by L.A's alternative metal giants, Tool, shows the band taking their sound to a more progressive style compared to their debut album. This heavy and emotional release, shows off the groundwork being laid down by the band to achieve their progressive sound we'd hear in later releases. Each member of the band is freakishly talented which is clear to hear on the record, whether it's Danny Carey's mind blowing polyrhythms on drums or Justin Chancellor's unique bass riffs... read more >
